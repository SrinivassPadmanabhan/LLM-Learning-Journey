Acme Corp Q3 2025 Business Review
Author: Jane Doe, CFO
Date: October 10, 2025

1. Executive Summary
Q3 was a strong quarter overall. We exceeded targets in two of three business units. 
The main headwind was increased cloud infrastructure costs. However, this was offset by enterprise sales growth.

2. Product Updates
2.1 Analytics Dashboard v3
We shipped the new analytics dashboard last week. User feedback has been positive so far.
The load time decreased by 40% compared to v2. It is now the fastest dashboard in the market.
This improvement was driven by the new caching layer. The team is proud of the result.

2.2 Mobile App
The iOS app is still in beta. Android release is scheduled for Q4.
It has 2,000 daily active users. They report better stability than v1.
The main complaint is login time. We will address this in the next sprint.

3. Financial Performance
3.1 Revenue
Our Q3 revenue hit $2.1M, up 18% YoY. The main driver was enterprise contracts.
Enterprise now represents 65% of total revenue, up from 52% last year.
It increased due to the new dashboard improving sales demos. This also reduced churn in the SMB segment.

3.2 Costs
Total cloud spend was $420k, a 30% increase QoQ. This was unexpected.
The primary cause was inefficient queries from the legacy reporting tool.
We are migrating users off it in Q4. That should bring costs back down.

3.3 Headcount
We hired 12 engineers in Q3. The total team size is now 84.
They are primarily focused on the data platform. It is critical for Q4 goals.

4. Risks and Mitigation
- Risk 1: Cloud costs. Mitigation: Finish migration, add query caps.
- Risk 2: Hiring competition. Mitigation: Increase referral bonus to $10k.
- Risk 3: SMB churn. It improved in Q3 but remains a watch item.
